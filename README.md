# Digital Marketing in Aviation: Analysis of Airline Strategies in the UK & Ireland

In-depth research and data analysis project evaluating digital marketing strategies used by major airlines in the UK and Ireland as part of a Master’s at South East Technological University (SETU). A mixed-methods approach (quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews) was used to generate data-driven insights and strategic recommendations for airline digital marketing.

## Methodology
### 1. Mixed-Methods Approach The study utilized a combination of quantitative and qualitative research methods.
### 2. Quantitative research: 208 survey responses collected via Google Forms using 17 structured questions distributed through email and WhatsApp.
### 3. Qualitative research: 3 semi-structured interviews conducted with 2 digital marketing professionals and 1 frequent flyer.

## Data Collection
Google Forms, Email Surveys, WhatsApp 

## Tools & Technologies
Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, SciPy), Jupyter Notebook, Anaconda, Microsoft Excel, Google Forms.

## Analysis Techniques
Statistical analysis and thematic analysis for interviews.

## Research Frameworks
PESTLE Analysis, Porter's Five Forces, SWOT Analysis, Thematic Analysis 

## Results
-79.8% of respondents were aged 18–34, showing strong representation of digitally active travelers.
-Airline websites and OTAs were the most influential booking channels.
-41.3% were aged 25–34, while 38.5% were 18–24.
-Personalized marketing increased engagement and booking intent among younger and middle-aged travelers.
-Real-time updates, chatbots, and proactive communication improved customer recommendations.
-Digital customer experience (208 respondents) significantly improved satisfaction and repeat travel intent.
-Loyalty program members showed higher engagement and travel frequency than non-members.

## Key Recommendation
Airlines should prioritize AI-driven personalization, real-time customer communication, and integrated omnichannel experiences to improve customer satisfaction, retention, and long-term competitive advantage.
